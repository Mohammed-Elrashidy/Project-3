Reasons To Read Books

Mental Stimulation & Cognitive Benefits. Reading keeps your brain active and engaged, helping to improve memory, focus, and problem-solving skills. It can delay cognitive decline as you age and increase your vocabulary and comprehension.

Escape & Stress Relief Books offer a form of escapism, allowing you to immerse yourself in different worlds, cultures, and perspectives. They can be a powerful way to relax and reduce stress, providing a break from daily pressures.

Personal Growth & Empathy. Reading expands your knowledge, opens your mind to new ideas, and helps you develop a deeper understanding of other people's experiences. It enhances empathy by allowing you to see the world through the eyes of different characters.

Audio Books
Fourth wing

Fourth Wing by Rebecca Yarros follows the story of Violet Sorrengail, a young woman forced by her mother to join the Riders Quadrant at Basgiath War College. Despite her physical frailty, Violet must train to become a dragon rider in a brutal and highly competitive environment. The plot delves into her struggle for survival, alliances, and unexpected romantic entanglements, especially with Xaden Riorson, the son of a rebel leader.

 Published October 31, 2012

Lights Out

"Lights Out" by Natalie Walters is a suspenseful thriller and the first installment in The SNAP Agency series. The story follows CIA analyst Brynn Taylor, who has developed a program aimed at combating terrorism. She hosts a conference involving international intelligence agents, but things take a dangerous turn when an Egyptian spy, Remon Riad, goes missing.

 Published September 11, 2007

What We Hide

"What We Hide" by Marthe Jocelyn is a compelling young adult novel set during the Vietnam War era. The story unfolds at a British boarding school, where several teenagers grapple with their own secrets and personal issues. The book is told from multiple perspectives, each offering a unique narrative style like letters, diary entries, and screenplay formats, adding a distinctive voice to each character’s story.

 Published May 21, 1999
